Nobody expects a trip to end with 14 people stranded at the airport.

For a group that had spent days solving robotics problems, missing a flight was definitely not the challenge we expected to fail.

What made it even crazier was how calm everyone looked for the first few minutes, like maybe the flight would wait for us out of respect.

But looking back, that moment perfectly captured the whole vibe of the trip: unexpected, a bit chaotic, and totally unforgettable.

What started as a trip to the AI For Good Robotics Competition turned into one of the best experiences our team has ever shared.

The trip was a whirlwind. Between late-night practice sessions and quick prayers before the big moments, the team found time to hang out, play table tennis, and just bond. It wasn't just about the robots; it was about the team becoming a family.

We got to the venue early, only to find out the arena layout was a bit different than what we'd practiced for. Instead of panicking, the students jumped right in, tweaking their code on the spot to make sure the robots were perfectly aligned.

With 22 teams from across the country, the competition was stiff. But our kids stood out immediately. While a lot of teams used standard kits, our students showed up with custom-built robots they designed themselves. Even the international observers from the ITU were impressed, calling their work "advanced" and "brilliant."

The competition was intense. Team SMAIR fought through a tough scoring deduction but still managed to snag 3rd place overall! But we didn't just win with robots. Our students absolutely cleaned up in the academic rounds, winning the AI, Robotics, and General Knowledge quizzes. Watching them stand on that stage and take home trophy after trophy was easily the proudest moment of the entire trip.

Just when everyone thought the adventure was over, Abuja had one last surprise waiting for us. Because of delays while checking out from the hotel, the team arrived too late and missed the scheduled flight home. Yes. All 14 of us.

At first, we were stressed, but the airport eventually just became another place to laugh and swap stories.

The coolest part? While we were waiting, we bumped into some dignitaries, including the vice chancellor of Bells University. They'd already heard the news about our 3rd-place win and stopped to congratulate the kids. Even a missed flight turned into a win for their confidence.

Between the trophies and the "airport campout," we saw these kids grow so much. They learned how to handle pressure, how to pivot when things go wrong, and how to have each other's backs.

At SMAIR Foundation, this is what it's all about. It's not just coding. It's about building leaders. We came home with medals and prizes, but the stories we'll be telling for years are the real win. And honestly? Missing that flight might have been the perfect ending to the story.
